Prooemium

Salvete, lectores! Hic liber demonstrationis causa creatus est. Lingua Latina, quamquam antiqua, adhuc vivit in cordibus multorum hominum qui eius pulchritudinem et elegantiam admirantur.

(Greetings, readers! This book was created for demonstration purposes. The Latin language, although ancient, still lives in the hearts of many people who admire its beauty and elegance.)

Caput I: De Lingua Latina

Lingua Latina est lingua Italica quae originem ducit ex Latio antiquo. Per multa saecula, haec lingua fuit vehiculum culturae, scientiae, philosophiae, religionis et iuris in Europa occidentali.

(The Latin language is an Italic language that originates from ancient Latium. For many centuries, this language was the vehicle of culture, science, philosophy, religion, and law in Western Europe.)

Hodie, etsi non est lingua quotidiani usus, Latina adhuc docetur in scholis et universitatibus. Multi homines eam discunt ut opera litteraria, philosophica et scientifica antiqua legere possint.

(Today, although it is not a language of daily use, Latin is still taught in schools and universities. Many people learn it to be able to read ancient literary, philosophical, and scientific works.)

Caput II: Fabulae Breves

De Corvo et Vulpe

Corvus caseum rostro tenebat. Vulpes, caseum appetens, sic allocuta est: "O corve, quam pulcher es! Si vox tua tam pulchra est quam forma tua, avium regina es." Corvus, laudibus inflatus, cantare voluit et caseum e rostro demisit. Vulpes caseum rapuit et dixit: "O corve, omnia habes; tibi deest tantum mens."

(A crow was holding cheese in its beak. A fox, desiring the cheese, addressed it thus: "O crow, how beautiful you are! If your voice is as beautiful as your form, you are the queen of birds." The crow, inflated by the praise, wanted to sing and dropped the cheese from its beak. The fox snatched the cheese and said: "O crow, you have everything; you only lack intelligence.")

Caput III: Sententiae et Proverbia

Carpe diem. (Horatius)

(Seize the day.)

Cogito, ergo sum. (Descartes)

(I think, therefore I am.)

Veni, vidi, vici. (Iulius Caesar)

(I came, I saw, I conquered.)

Ars longa, vita brevis. (Hippocrates)

(Art is long, life is short.)

Errare humanum est, perseverare autem diabolicum. (Seneca)

(To err is human, but to persist is diabolical.)
